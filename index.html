Why a good business card still matters

In a world of digital contacts and QR codes, a well-designed business card remains one of the most effective networking tools you can carry. It is tangible, memorable, and gives the person you meet something physical to associate with your name.

A business card does more than share a phone number. It communicates professionalism, reinforces your brand, and signals that you take your work seriously. When you hand someone a clean, thoughtfully designed card, you are making a small promise about the quality of everything else you do.

What makes an effective business card?

  • Clarity first. Your name, role and primary contact method should be readable at a glance. Avoid cramming in every detail.
  • Consistent branding. Use colours and fonts that match your logo, website and other materials so people recognise you instantly.
  • Breathing room. Generous white space makes a card feel premium. A cluttered card feels cheap, no matter how nice the content is.
  • One clear focal point. Usually your name or company. Everything else supports it.
  • Print quality. A blurry, low-resolution print undoes all your design effort. Always export at 300 DPI or higher.

Absolutely. The image uploader works for both photos and logos. After uploading, use the built-in editor to crop and position the image.
